How New York Embraced Mobile Wagering

by Jake Lang

November 10th, 2023

New York, NY — Sports betting is still federally illegal, but as far as the states are concerned, the money is too good. After initially launching statewide in January 2022, there are now nine options for consumers looking to place wagers, including FanDuel, DraftKings, Caesar's, Bet MGM, Rush Interactive, Bally's, Wynn, Points Bet, and Resorts World. Of course, like in all wagering, though, there will still be scores of winners and losers-- New York State, the various mobile sports wagering platforms, and the people are all both, depending on who you ask.

An all-time look at the amount of money being wagered through their platforms shows just how much these platforms are winning. Just look at these charts below and run the numbers yourself with the original data set. here.

Add up the individual platform totals and you'll get a cool $29.1 billion for New York State's total mobile sports betting handle in just under a full 23 months since launch. Odd are, mobile sports wagering isn’t going anywhere soon.
